Why BEAT exists

Competing has never been more expensive.
The coffees worth it, further out of reach.

The average barista on the World stage spent $10,000–$12,000 of their own money in 2025 — before even buying their competition coffee. The cost of pursuing a craft has multiplied. Sponsors and roaster partners do incredible work. We want to do our part too.

When Regina Tay brewed for Singapore at the 2016 WBC, she used our coffee. So did Stefanos Paterakis for Greece. So did Lise Marie Rømo for Norway. Over the next decade, more than twenty champions and finalists from a dozen countries have brewed La Palma & El Tucán on the world's biggest stages.

A thank-you note, written in green coffee.

BEAT exists because the specialty coffee industry took a chance on us when we were a young finca with one ferment tank, no auction record, and a Sidra varietal nobody outside Cundinamarca had heard of.

The roasters who paid us a fair price for our first lots. The baristas who put our coffees on their competition cards. The importers who said yes before we had a website. They built this farm with us — and to them, this is our most direct way of saying thank you.

BEAT is how we pay it forward to the next generation of competitors who haven't yet won a regional, who won't be bidding at auction, but who deserve a coffee good enough to take to a final.

A note from Felipe

On trust, and the people who give it.

This picture is from the day Jooyeon Jeon won the 2019 World Barista Championship in Boston. She used a coffee from our farm — no sponsorship, no contract, no logo on her stage. She picked our Sidra because it was good, and then she went on stage and made the world taste it.

That moment didn't just put La Palma & El Tucán on a map. It humbled us. Because Jooyeon — like Cole, like Regina, like Sebastián, like every competitor before and after them — bet a year of preparation on a single producer's harvest. On us. That kind of trust is rare, and it's what built this farm.

BEAT is new ground for us. Every coffee on the Wall of Fame above was bought — by the competitors, by their roasters, by the teams who pieced together what it took to get a lot of LP&ET on stage. They earned every cup, and we're grateful to all of them. With BEAT, we're trying something different for today's and tomorrow's competitors: 20 boxes a year, awarded to baristas whose craft deserves a coffee that isn't behind an auction paddle. We just want to be one less thing standing between you and the moment you've trained for.

Felipe Co-founder · La Palma & El Tucán

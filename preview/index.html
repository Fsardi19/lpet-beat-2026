Why BEAT exists

Competing has never been more expensive.
Most producers don't help.

Coffee competitions used to be a barista's craft on display. Today they're an industry — sponsored, scored, and increasingly out of reach. The cost of a single regional barista campaign now averages $8,000 USD before travel. Most producers leave their competitors to figure it out alone.

"We grew the coffee. We processed it. We roasted it. The least we can do is hand it back to the person who's going to put it on a stage."
— Felipe Sardi · Co-founder

What we don't look for

Hyped editing. Flashy gear. Perfect English. A polished elevator pitch you'd give a venture capitalist.

We've seen 800 submissions across two editions. The ones we remember had a 5-second moment where the barista forgot the camera and made coffee. Find that moment.

A note from Felipe

Why we back the people on stage.

I'm in this picture with Jooyeon Jeon, the day she won the 2019 World Barista Championship. She used a coffee from our farm. We didn't pay for placement. We didn't sponsor a campaign.

She picked it because it was good. And then she went on stage and made the world taste it.

BEAT is our way of saying: if you're going to put a Geisha or a Sidra on a stage, you should know the people who grew it, fermented it, and stayed up at night drying it. We won't be on the stage with you. But we'll be the reason you're memorable.

Felipe Co-founder · La Palma & El Tucán
